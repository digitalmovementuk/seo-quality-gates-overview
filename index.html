STAGE 0

Before any page exists — keyword scope

Bad keywords never become pages. These run on the keyword list, not on HTML.

G0·1Keyword scope gateDROPPED

What it checks · No competitor, brand or company-name keywords (legal forms like GmbH / AG, a brand denylist, &-brands). Off-topic word collisions get flagged for a human to review, not auto-dropped.

Why it matters

Someone searching a rival's company name will never become your customer — they already know who they want. Building pages for those terms burns time and money on traffic that never turns into an enquiry.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Google rewards pages that match what the searcher actually wants. A page targeting a competitor's brand can't satisfy that, so it won't rank — and the dead pages dilute the authority of the pages that should.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Hand Claude the raw keyword list and ask it to flag any term containing a legal form (GmbH, AG, Ltd), a known competitor or brand name, or an &-brand — and to split "clearly navigational" from "ambiguous" so a human only reviews the grey-area terms.

G0·2SERP consultancy gateDROPPED

What it checks · Every kept keyword must show consultancies / agencies in Google's top 3 and top 10 (Google only, via Semrush SERP — never Bing or DuckDuckGo).

Why it matters

The current top results tell you who Google thinks the searcher wants. If it shows tools, Wikipedia or courses instead of firms like yours, a service page has almost no chance there — better to know that before you build it.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Matching the dominant result type ("search intent") is one of the strongest ranking signals. Targeting keywords where real firms already rank means you're entering a race you can actually win.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Give Claude a SERP export per keyword and have it label the top 10 by type (consultancy / tool / info article / directory). Keep only keywords where service firms appear in both the top 3 and the wider top 10.

G0·3Buying-intent filterDROPPED

What it checks · Only keywords with real buying intent for your service stay (service terms, "consultant", locations, pricing). How-tos, definitions and DIY queries are dropped.

Why it matters

A "how to do X myself" searcher wants to avoid hiring you; a "X consultant near me" searcher wants to hire. Your pages should target the second group.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Pages aimed at buyers earn the clicks and enquiries that signal relevance and quality to Google, lifting rankings. Info pages bring traffic that bounces without ever converting.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude to sort each keyword into "ready to buy", "comparing options" or "just researching / DIY", and drop the last bucket unless it's a deliberate top-of-funnel play.

G0·4EYS enquiry triageRESERVE

What it checks · Ranks the keywords that passed by expected enquiries per month, with a cap per service cluster → a finite build list (default 400) plus a reserve pool.

Why it matters

You can't build everything at once. This puts the keywords most likely to generate real enquiries at the front of the queue.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Concentrating effort on the highest-yield pages (and not flooding one topic) builds topical authority faster and avoids the thin, competing pages that drag a whole site down.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude estimate monthly enquiry potential per keyword (volume × intent × win-likelihood), apply a per-cluster cap so one service doesn't eat the budget, and output a ranked build list with the rest held in reserve.

STAGE 1

Per-page audit chain — runs automatically, in order

A page is never "done" after one gate — the whole chain runs in one command and reports a single consolidated verdict.

1Benchmark gateBLOCKER

What it checks · Structure + detail vs the depth benchmark (body 1,500–1,800 words). Blocks any claim to hold certificates or own tools (U1a/U1b), scans for invented data (D1), and requires industry frameworks to be explained, never claimed as owned.

Why it matters

Thin or padded pages, or pages that fib about credentials, read as untrustworthy and get ignored by buyers — and can drag the whole site into trouble.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Google's "helpful content" system rewards genuinely useful, appropriately deep pages and demotes thin or misleading ones. Hitting the right depth with honest claims keeps you on the right side of it.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude to measure body word count, list every credential / tool / statistic claim and check each is true and supported, and confirm framework mentions explain the method rather than claim ownership — then fix or cut whatever fails.

2Substance blocks (4 + 1)BLOCKER

What it checks · Hub pages carry exactly five human-written blocks — misdiagnosis · methodology · outcome · cost · market. No AI filler, no missing block, no empty placeholder.

Why it matters

Real first-hand experience and specifics are what make a page believable. These five blocks force genuine human insight onto every hub.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Google's E-E-A-T framework (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) favours pages with real first-hand substance — exactly the AI-only filler it tries to demote.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Use Claude to draft the questions that pull these five stories out of the client, mark each block "human insert", and refuse to publish until all five are filled with real content.

2bGoogle Essentials (G1–G11)BLOCKER

What it checks · Google's own published guidance: honest title/H1 · who-created-it shown · honest dates · original value · no placeholders/sloppiness · no padding · descriptive title · sources cited · no keyword stuffing · not thin · no hidden text.

Why it matters

These are the basics Google says it judges every page on. Failing them is an own goal.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Meeting Search Essentials keeps a page eligible to rank at all; tripping a spam policy can suppress or de-index it.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Give Claude the page plus Google's Search Essentials checklist and have it return a pass/fail per rule with the exact fix — e.g. rewrite a clickbait title to match the content, add an author, remove stuffed phrases.

336-check gate-runnerBLOCKER

What it checks · 20 compliance + 16 ranking checks (full list in the next section). Refuses to write sitemap.xml until every page is green.

Why it matters

It's the one automated pass that catches the dozens of small technical and content issues a human eye slides past.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Each check maps to a known ranking or indexing factor; clearing all 36 means no obvious technical reason is holding the page back.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Point Claude at the page and the 36-check list, have it run each check, report fails with fixes, and re-check after edits until all 36 are green.

3bAI-Search / GEO gateBLOCKER

What it checks · Citation-readiness for ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews. Threshold ≥ 85/100. No snake oil — it rewards structure, extractability and entity consistency.

Why it matters

A growing share of searches now end inside an AI answer. If the AI can't easily lift a clean fact from your page, you're invisible to those users.

How it lifts your Google ranking

The same structure that earns AI citations (a clear answer up top, tidy headings, specific facts) also helps classic rankings — it's the opposite of a gimmick.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude whether it could answer the target question using only this page, and where it would struggle — then tighten the opening answer, headings and facts until extraction is effortless.

4Content relevance (R16)BLOCKER

What it checks · The copy is built from, and covers, the page's research corpus — real Reddit threads + People-Also-Ask: actual questions, pain points, terminology. No corpus → no copy → no ship.

Why it matters

Writing from real user language guarantees the page answers the questions people actually ask, in their own words.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Covering the real questions and terms around a topic builds the topical completeness Google rewards, and earns featured snippets and AI citations.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude mine Reddit + PAA for the topic, extract the recurring questions and phrasing, and check the draft answers each one. Add a section for any gap before publishing.

5seo-page-benchmark (LLM scorecard)100/100

What it checks · An LLM scorecard across nine weighted areas — Indexability · Content Quality · Schema · E-E-A-T · Internal Linking · Accessibility · Performance · AI Readiness · Conversion. Hard pass bar: 100/100.

Why it matters

It's the final all-round health check; 100/100 means nothing important was left half-done.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Every dimension it scores is a real ranking or conversion lever, so a perfect score is a page firing on all of them.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Run the page through Claude as a scorer, get the 0–100 per dimension with the gap list, fix the lowest scores first, and re-score until it reads 100/100.

STANDALONE · 5 GATESrun alongside the chain

S1Top-bar matchBLOCKER

What it checks · The page nav matches the site homepage exactly (style, buttons, logo — desktop + mobile); only the in-page jump-links adapt to the page.

Why it matters

A consistent header makes the page feel like a real part of the site, not a bolted-on landing page — which builds trust and keeps people browsing.

How it lifts your Google ranking

A coherent site structure and navigation help Google understand the site and keep users engaged (lower bounce) — both positive signals.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude copy the homepage nav's HTML/CSS/JS into the page, swap only the section anchor links, then diff the two headers to confirm they match.

S2UX gate (mobile + readability)BLOCKER

What it checks · UX-1 mobile: centred layout, section spacing, viewport meta, 16px form inputs (so iPhones don't zoom). UX-2 readability: sensible line length, body text size, heading order.

Why it matters

Most visitors are on a phone. If it's awkward to read or tap, they leave before they enquire.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Mobile usability and page experience are direct Google ranking factors — and a clean, readable page also converts far better.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude to audit the page at phone width for tap-target size, font sizes, spacing and heading order, and apply the CSS fixes for anything that fails.

S3De-invent gateBLOCKER

What it checks · Strips or flags any fabricated numbers, stats, years, client counts or prices — the never-invent-data rule applied to copy, schema, meta and alt text.

Why it matters

One made-up statistic destroys trust and can be legally risky. A real business must only state facts it can stand behind.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Google's trust signals penalise inaccurate content; invented "facts" also can't be backed by sources, which weakens E-E-A-T.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude list every concrete number / claim and label each "client-supplied & verified" or "unverified" — then remove or soften the unverified ones to qualitative wording.

S4Section distinctnessBLOCKER

What it checks · Every section owns a distinct angle — no two sections repeating the same concepts. Compares sections mathematically (TF-IDF cosine) and blocks duplicates, weak-focus sections and clashing "signatures".

Why it matters

Pages that say the same thing five different ways feel padded and waste the reader's time.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Distinct, non-repetitive sections cover more of the topic and avoid the "thin / padded" signals the helpful-content system demotes.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude to summarise each section in one line, flag any pair that overlaps too much, and rewrite the weaker one to cover a genuinely different angle.

S5Visual QA (agent)BLOCKER

What it checks · Screenshots + self-critiques the page at desktop AND mobile. The hero must fit the first screen (1440×900) and the hero eyebrow + lead must talk about the same topic as the H1.

Why it matters

First impressions are visual. A broken or off-topic hero loses people in the first two seconds.

How it lifts your Google ranking

A focused, on-topic hero and clean layout keep visitors on the page (engagement signals) and reinforce relevance for the target term.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude take desktop and mobile screenshots, check the hero fits and stays on-topic with the H1, and list every visual issue to fix before sign-off.

STAGE 2

Whole-set gates — before the sitemap ships

Per-page green is not enough; the set has to be healthy as a whole.

W1Index-readiness gateScore ≥ 50

What it checks · The whole set for near-duplicates and unique substance, gives a "fewer-but-stronger-pages" verdict, and prints a Uniqueness Score per page.

Why it matters

Publishing hundreds of similar pages backfires — most stay unindexed. This catches that before launch.

How it lifts your Google ranking

Google indexes and ranks distinct, substantial pages; near-duplicate sets get mostly ignored or filtered.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Ask Claude to score each page's uniqueness across the set and recommend which thin / duplicate pages to merge or cut.

W2Uniqueness thresholdboth conditions

What it checks · Each cluster's median uniqueness ≥ 50% AND ≥ 80% of pages individually pass — validated on a 5–8-page sample before building the full set.

Why it matters

It proves the template can actually produce unique pages before you write hundreds of them.

How it lifts your Google ranking

A set that clears this bar avoids the near-duplicate filtering that kills programmatic SEO.

How a specialist implements it with Claude

Have Claude build a small sample first, measure uniqueness, and only greenlight the full build if both conditions hold.
